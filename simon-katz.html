Simon Katz Biography

Simon Katz is one of the most popular and richest Musician who was born on May 16, 1971 in Nottingham, England, United Kingdom. From 2002 until 2003, Katz was the music director of Ms. Dynamite, during which she was awarded the Mercury Prize for her debut album, A Little Deeper; two Brit Awards which included Best British Female Artist, and three MOBO Awards.

Katz sometimes works on film. He was featured in the music scores in the 2002 documentary City of God and the document …More Than 1,000 Words and also served as music supervisor for his 2008 movie Ball Don’t Lie.

The year 2001 saw Katz was a member of Damon Albarn’s band Gorillaz, a group of cartoons featuring real musicians who provide the vocals and instruments for their animated “band members.” On live performances as well as on the 2002 album of remixes Laika Come Home, he played the guitar in the role of Noodle the 14-year-old Japanese schoolgirl who was a guitarist prodigy. Katz was in the 2008 movie Bananaz that chronicles the evolution of Gorillaz from 2000 to 2006.
