Will Dricus Du Plessis Fight Sean Strickland again in 2024?

The highly anticipated rematch between Dricus Du Plessis and Sean Strickland has been a topic of much discussion within the MMA community.

Following Strickland's controversial split-decision loss to Du Plessis at UFC 297, the possibility of a rematch has sparked considerable interest.

In this comprehensive article, Fan Arch will closely look into various aspects and factors that may influence a potential rematch between these two formidable fighters.

The Controversial Title Loss and Rematch Demand

Sean Strickland, the former UFC middleweight champion, suffered a contentious split-decision loss to Dricus Du Plessis in his first title defense at UFC 297.

This defeat has led Strickland to express his desire for an immediate rematch with Du Plessis, citing his belief that he deserved the victory based on his performance and the statistics of the fight.

Strickland's strong assertion for a rematch is evident in his public statements and social media posts, where he has adamantly called for the opportunity to run it back with Du Plessis.

The UFC's Stance and Corporate Interests

Despite Strickland's vocal demand for a rematch, the UFC's stance on the matter remains a point of interest.

UFC CEO Dana White expressed his view that he had the fight tied going into the fifth round and scored the final round in favor of Strickland.

However, the UFC's attempt to book Du Plessis against Israel Adesanya for the UFC 300 main event indicates that the promotion may have other plans for the next 185-pound title bout. 

This raises questions about the extent to which corporate interests and matchmaking decisions within the UFC may influence the likelihood of a rematch between Strickland and Du Plessis.

Fighter Autonomy and Freedom of Expression

Strickland's frustration post-title loss reflects a broader tension between fighters' freedom of expression and corporate interests in MMA.

His accusations of the UFC avoiding an immediate rematch to stifle his unfiltered personality shed light on the delicate balance between athlete autonomy and commercial interests in the sport.

This aspect adds a layer of complexity to the discussion surrounding the potential rematch, as it intertwines the fighters' personal expressions with the promotional decisions made by the UFC.

The Case for a Rematch

From a competitive standpoint, the case for a rematch between Du Plessis and Strickland is compelling.

Strickland's impressive victory over Israel Adesanya to claim the middleweight belt at UFC 293, followed by his subsequent loss to Du Plessis, showcases the competitive nature of the division and underscores the potential for an exhilarating rematch.

Whether the interest is there or not for the casual fans, MMA aficionados would certainly love to see Strickland and Du Plessis clash in the octagon once again.

Furthermore, Strickland's prior accomplishments and his assertion that he deserves another opportunity to compete for the title significantly contribute to the argument for a rematch.

Du Plessis' Perspective and Intentions

Understanding Dricus Du Plessis' perspective and intentions regarding a potential rematch is crucial in assessing the likelihood of the fight taking place.

While there may be speculation about Du Plessis defending his middleweight belt against Stylebender, the heated face-off between Du Plessis and Strickland at UFC 290 and the demand for the rematch by Strickland indicate the possibility of a showdown between these two formidable fighters.

The Future of the Middleweight Division

The potential rematch between Dricus Du Plessis and Sean Strickland has significant implications for the UFC's middleweight division.

It not only presents an opportunity for Strickland to seek redemption but also serves as a pivotal moment in shaping the competitive landscape of the division.

The outcome of this potential rematch could have far-reaching effects on the rankings, future title bouts, and the overall narrative of the middleweight division in 2024 and beyond.

The controversies surrounding their initial encounter, the fighters' respective demands and intentions, as well as the broader dynamics of athlete autonomy and corporate interests within the UFC, all contribute to the intrigue surrounding this potential rematch.

Not to mention that the middleweight division in the UFC is emerging as one of the best and most marketable divisions as of late, so fan interest should definitely follow.

As the MMA community eagerly awaits further developments, the possibility of witnessing Du Plessis and Strickland share the Octagon once again remains a tantalizing prospect that could redefine the landscape of the UFC's middleweight division in 2024.
